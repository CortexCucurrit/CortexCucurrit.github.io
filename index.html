Getting to Maine

If you're coming from New England, we recommend you drive:

Mount Desert Island is a five hour drive from Boston (four and a half if you drive like you're from Massachusetts...) If you have several extra hours, consider taking coastal route 1 between Brunswick and Ellsworth to see small town coastal Maine. When we drive up (usually after work on a Friday) we stop for dinner at The Highroller Lobster Company in Portland, Maine or The Quarry Taproom in Hallowell, Maine.

If you plan to fly:

The three major airports closest to Mount Desert Island are:

  1. Bangor International Airport, 1 hour away from Mount Desert Island (we recommend using this airport)
  2. The Hancock County-Bar Harbor Airport, 5 minutes from Mount Desert Island
  3. Boston Logan International Airport, 5 hours from Mount Desert Island

If you plan to fly, we recommend that you fly into and out of Bangor International Airport. The Hancock County-Bar Harbor Airport is closer to Mount Desert Island but sometimes unreliable; flights can be cancelled due to fog. If you fly into Boston, we recommend that you rent a car and drive the rest of the way to Maine (~5 hours).
If you fly into Bangor, you should either rent a car (we recommend this option) or schedule airport transfers via the Bar Harbor Shuttle. Transfers must be scheduled at least 72 hours in advance (and we recommend calling weeks in advance as these seats fill up.)
The Portland International Jetport in Portland, Maine and the Manchester-Boston regional airport are alternatives to the airports listed above. You should plan on renting a car and driving the remaining distance to the island if you opt for either of these airports.

If you plan to take a bus:

Concord Coach runs several busses a day between Boston's South Station and Bangor, which is an hour away from Mount Desert Island. To get to the island from Bangor, book a ride on the Bar Harbor Shuttle (we recommend calling several weeks in advance at least to reserve your seat.) More information on getting to the island by bus can be found here.

Things to Do

Walks & Hikes We Do with Our Parents

  1. Hunters Brook Trail: 3.6 miles round trip, leads to a secluded pebbled beach.
  2. Great Head: 1.6 miles round trip, leads to a former lighthouse with great views. National Park Pass required; arrive by 8:30 am to get a parking spot.
  3. Lower Hadlock Pond: 2.3 miles round trip, quiet reservoir with lots of tadpoles.

Our Favorite Hikes (these are a work out)

  1. Sargent & Penobscot Mountains: Park at the Jordan Pond House, hike down the east side of Jordan Pond to the Deer Brook trail. Follow signs for Sargent Mountain summit. Once you've made it to the top of Sargent, follow signs for Penobscot Mountain summit. If you have time, stop to eat popovers on the Jordan Pond House lawn before you leave. 3 hour hike, National Park Pass required; arrive by 9am to get a parking space.
  2. Pemetic Mountain: Park at the Jordan Pond House, hike down the east side of Jordan Pond to the Carry trail. Cross the Park Loop Road and hike up the Pemetic Ravine trail. Come down the Pemetic South Ridge trail. 2.5 hour hike, National Park Pass required, arrive by 9am to get a parking space.
  3. Acadia & St. Sauveur Mountains: Park at the Acadia Mountain parking lot on Route 102. Hike up to the Acadia Mountain summit, then follow signs for St. Sauveur Mountain summit/East Face Trail. Follow signs for the Acadia Mountain parking lot on your way down. 3 hour hike.

Our Favorite Restaurants

Breakfast

  • Jordan's Restaurant, Bar Harbor. A Maine institution; Grace's grandfather brought her mom here on weekend mornings. We get the same order they did: the Pancake Club special with blueberry pancakes and real maple syrup.
  • Cafe This Way, Bar Harbor. We recommend the omelets.
  • The Colonel's Restaurant and Bakery, Northeast Harbor. The bakery is the star of the show here; the chocolate and blueberry glazed donuts are incredible!
  • Choco-Latte, Bar Harbor. Good breakfast and coffee to-go.

Lunch & Dinner

  • Jordan Pond House Acadia National Park. Parking is difficult but this is an Acadia institution. Public transportation via the Island Explorer is available to Jordan Pond House. Ask for a table on the lawn as close to the water as possible. The clam chowder, cheese board, and popovers with prosecco are all good options. Reservations strongly recommended.
  • The Travelin Lobster, Bar Harbor. The best lobster on the island. Outdoor seating only; bring bug juice. Owned by a Bar Harbor lobsterman, the lobster comes straight off his boat in the morning. We recommend finding a friend and ordering the lobster dinner for two (or you could just eat two lobsters...)
  • The Asticou Inn porch, Northeast Harbor. Ask for a table on the porch with a view of the water; the lobster rolls are the best thing on the menu here. Reservations recommended.
  • Abel's Lobster Pound, Town of Mount Desert. A nicer dinner option that's good for those who want to eat lobster indoors with non-plastic silverware and a view of the water. Reservations required; ask for a table indoors with a view of the water. We recommend the blueberry pie a la mode for dessert.
  • The Burning Tree, Otter Creek. Maine farm-to-table; vegetables are grown in the restaurant's backyard and nearby farms. If they're offered, the squash blossoms are incredible. Reservations recommended.
  • Havana, Bar Harbor. One of the nicest restaurants on Mount Desert Island. The paella is the star of the show, and the mojitos are awesome. Reservations required.

Ice Cream & Drinks

  • Mount Desert Island Ice Cream, Bar Harbor. Hands down the best place for ice cream on the island. The line is long, but it's worth it. There are two locations; we recommend the one close to the village green.
  • Atlantic Brewing Company, 15 Knox Road Bar Harbor. While Atlantic has a tasting room in downtown Bar Harbor, the Knox Road location is more fun. There's often live music on the weekends and tastings are free; our favorite is Brother Adam Braggart's Ale.

If you have a car:

  • Drive up to the Cadillac Mountain Summit (the first place in the United States where you can see the sun rise) and watch the sunrise or sunset. We recommend a hefty number of snacks and arriving at least an hour and fifteen minutes early; parking is limited. National park pass required.
  • Drive the Park Loop Road. Highlights include Thunder Hole, Cadillac Mountain, and Otter Cliffs. National park pass required.
  • Window shop in Bar Harbor and/or Northeast Harbor. Our favorites in Bar Harbor include Cool As A Moose, Bark Harbor, Sherman's Maine Coast Book Store, and In the Woods. Our favorites in Northeast Harbor are the Kimball Shop and Island Artisans.
  • Hike or Drive up to Thuya Garden and tour Thuya Lodge in Northeast Harbor. Stop by the Asticou Azalea Garden and take a stroll by the Japanese rock garden on your way out.
